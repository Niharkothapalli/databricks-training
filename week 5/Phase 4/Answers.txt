Reflection Questions – Answers :


1. Why is cleaning done before joining tables?

Cleaning is done before joining tables to remove incorrect, duplicate, and missing data. 
This helps produce accurate and reliable results after the join.



2. What would go wrong if null keys are not removed?

If null keys are not removed, some records will not match during the join. This can lead
to missing data, incorrect results, and incomplete reports.



3. How did you decide the join order?

I first joined the Orders table with the Customers table using the `customer_id` column. 
This allowed me to combine customer information with order details and perform the 
required analysis.



4. Which step was most difficult and why?

The final reporting table was the most difficult step because it required multiple joins and
aggregations. Combining all the required information into one DataFrame needed careful planning.



5. How is SQL logic similar to PySpark?

PySpark follows the same logic as SQL. Operations like SELECT, WHERE, GROUP BY, JOIN, and 
ORDER BY are available in PySpark through DataFrame functions.



6. What challenges will appear with large data?

Large datasets take more processing time and memory. They may also require optimization 
techniques such as partitioning and caching to improve performance.



7. Can you explain your pipeline in simple steps?

First, I loaded the customer and order datasets. Then I cleaned the data by removing invalid
records. After that, I joined the datasets, calculated business insights like daily sales 
and customer spending, segmented customers based on their total spend, created the final 
reporting table, and finally saved the output as a CSV file.
