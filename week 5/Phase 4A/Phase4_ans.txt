Reflection Questions :

- Why do we convert continuous values into categories?
- What is the difference between business segmentation and technical bucketing?
- When would fixed thresholds fail?
- How does quantile-based segmentation differ from fixed rules?
- Which method would you use in real-world projects?



Answers :

1. Why do we convert continuous values into categories?

We convert continuous values into categories to make the data easier to understand and 
analyze. Instead of looking at many different values, we can group them into categories 
like Gold, Silver, and Bronze. This helps businesses identify customer groups and make 
better decisions.




2. What is the difference between business segmentation and technical bucketing?

-Business segmentation uses fixed business rules to group customers, such as 
 Gold, Silver, and Bronze. 
-Technical bucketing groups data using mathematical or statistical methods instead of 
fixed business rules.

Business segmentation is mainly used for business decisions, while technical bucketing 
is often used in data analysis and machine learning.




3. When would fixed thresholds fail?

Fixed thresholds may fail when customer spending patterns change over time. For example, 
if most customers start spending more money, the old limits may no longer divide customers
fairly. In such cases, the business should update the thresholds or use another segmentation
method.




4. How does quantile-based segmentation differ from fixed rules?

Fixed rules always use the same spending limits to create customer groups. Quantile-based 
segmentation divides customers based on the actual distribution of the data, so each group 
has a similar number of customers. This makes quantile segmentation more flexible when the
data changes.




5. Which method would you use in real-world projects?

I would use Conditional Logic in most real-world projects because it is simple, easy to 
understand, and easy to implement. It follows business rules and clearly classifies customers
into groups like Gold, Silver, and Bronze. This makes it useful for customer segmentation,
business reports, loyalty programs, and marketing campaigns..
